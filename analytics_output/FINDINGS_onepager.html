1 · What the daily data shows — a strong-looking pattern

Every setup is a qualified institutional footprint (volume ≥ 3× ADV, move ≥ 2 ATR, close in the extreme quartile). On Day 2, 73% gapped in the continuation direction; a fresh catalyst lifts that to 87% (93% for bullish earnings), and the pattern held every month. On paper, a clean edge.

2 · The catch — most of that "edge" is untradable

That continuation is the overnight gap — it happens while you are flat. Measured from a tradable open entry, the long side inverts: buying the Day-2 open of a bullish setup wins only 44% of the time (−0.8% median), and the celebrated bullish-earnings cell wins just 31%. The 93% was the gap, not a trade.

3 · The resolution — the entry rule is the edge

The methodology is explicit: don't buy the open — wait for the morning Test and the Character Flip. Backtesting that exact rule (enter on the flip, stop below the turn) turns the long side from a loser into +1.66R and shorts into +1.46R — +1.56R overall. Discipline at the entry, not the setup alone, is where the money is.

VERDICT — Promising, not yet proven. The descriptive pattern is real, but the headline continuation is mostly an untradable overnight gap. The tradable edge lives entirely in the playbook's flip entry — and the pilot supports it (+1.56R). It must be re-run at scale with costs before it is bankable.

Caveats

  • n = 51 pilot (sub-cells ~13) — directional, not conclusive.
  • No costs yet — commission, spread, and short-borrow on small caps are unmodeled.
  • "Clean-hold" trades have tiny stops, which inflate R-multiples.
  • Universe may carry selection bias; no control group yet.
