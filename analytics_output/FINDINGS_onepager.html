1 · The daily data shows a strong pattern

Every stock here already cleared a strict filter for a genuine institutional move, meaning heavy volume, a wide range, and a close pinned at the extreme. The next day 73% gapped in the continuation direction, and a fresh catalyst lifts that to about 87%, with bullish earnings near 93%. The pattern showed up in every month, so on paper this looks like a clean, repeatable edge.

2 · The catch, most of that move is not tradable

The problem is that the continuation is mostly the overnight gap, which happens while you are flat and cannot act on it. If you measure from a realistic entry at the open instead, the long side actually flips negative. Buying the open of a bullish setup wins only about 44% of the time, and even the celebrated bullish earnings group wins just 31%. That headline 93% was the gap, not a trade you could take.

3 · The fix, the entry rule is the real edge

The playbook is explicit that you should not buy the open. You wait for the morning Test of a level and the Character Flip, then enter on that turn with a stop placed just beyond it. Backtesting that exact rule turns the long side from a loser into +1.66R and the short side into +1.46R, or about +1.56R overall. The discipline at entry, not the setup by itself, is where the money actually comes from.

VERDICT. Promising but not yet proven. The pattern is real, but the eye catching continuation is mostly an overnight gap you cannot trade. The part you can actually trade is the flip entry, and the pilot supports it at about +1.56R. Before treating this as money in the bank it needs to be run again at full scale with realistic costs included.

Caveats

  • The pilot is only 51 trades, with subgroups near 13, so it points a direction rather than proving one.
  • Costs are not included yet, so commission, spread, and borrow on small caps could eat into the result.
  • Some winners had very tight stops, which can flatter the R multiples.
  • The universe may carry selection bias, and there is no control group yet.
